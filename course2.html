About the Course

Computer networking classes combine lectures and hands-on practice to provide skills in computer network system configuration. Courses may include discussions, lectures and projects that deal with basic networking principles and current developments in the field. Computer networking courses train students to connect local area networks (LAN), wide area networks (WAN) and wireless versions of both types. They also learn to connect hardware devices and set up Internet access. These skills can apply to a variety of careers. According to the skills learned in networking classes are valuable to network engineers, programmers and consultants. Classes may be geared toward Unix or Windows systems and may lead toward certification in the field.
